Photo project

Transit Dreams: A Study of Sleep on the Commuter Train

As a photographer, one of my earliest personal projects was focused on capturing the sleeping train commuters traveling to and from New York City. This project was done before the pandemic, back when the world felt a little less chaotic and people's daily routines were more predictable. For me, there was something incredibly beautiful and peaceful about these moments of rest that passengers found on the train. Through my lens, I captured the stillness of each sleeper, providing a glimpse into the private moments that are often hidden in public spaces. Each photograph told a story, revealing the unique personalities and experiences of the commuters who trusted the train to take them to their destinations while they rested. Looking back, this project was an exercise in mindfulness, reminding me of the importance of slowing down and finding beauty in the everyday moments of life.

Photo project

Reclaimed Memories: Exploring Childhood Nostalgia Through Found Objects

I am currently working on a personal photography project that is deeply meaningful to me. As a child, I grew up in a turbulent household and missed out on many of the joys of childhood. Now, as an adult, I am exploring these missed opportunities through photography. Specifically, I am using found objects on the property of my old farm house as surrogates for memories I never had. Each object represents something that I longed for as a child, such as a treehouse, a tire swing, or a picnic table. By photographing these objects, I am able to bring these lost childhood memories to life and explore them in a way that feels both therapeutic and cathartic. It is a deeply personal project that is allowing me to process my past in a creative and healing way.
